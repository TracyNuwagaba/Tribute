Dr Maya Angelou

Maya Angelou, original name Marguerite Anne Johnson, (born April 4, 1928, St. Louis, Missouri, US — died May 28, 2014, Winston–Salem, North Carolina), was an American Poet, memoirist, screenwriter, dancer, actress and civil rights activist best known for her 1969 memoir, "I Know the Caged Bird Sings", which made literary history as the first nonfiction bestseller by an African American woman.

As a civil rights activist, Angelou worked for Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and Malcolm X. She was also an educator and served as the Reynolds professor of American Studies at Wake Forest University.
